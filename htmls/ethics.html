The Ethics of Computer-Generated Actors

Using CGI to “revive” actors is a very controversial topic.
This article begs the question of whether using CGI to “revive” deceased actors is ethical or not. On one hand, the actor cannot physically give consent to be a part of said movie/show and it’s overall a little odd and creepy to see someone who no longer exists on this planet act in a movie after their death. However, others see it in the way that it can honor the actor and that as long as the family consent to the action, it is perfectly okay. This is honestly a matter of personal beliefs and opinions but really shows just how far computer science has come.

What's being digitalized? What was the goal or purpose of digitalizing this thing?
Actor’s faces were digitized. The purpose of digitizing actor’s faces was to basically “revive” them through CGI after they have passed away in order to keep them in a film.
Is someone benefiting from this situation? If so, who? Is someone being harmed in this situation? If so, who?
The movie producers benefit and the fans benefit depending on whether they support these actions or not. This can also be a way for the actor’s family to cope with the grief. In a way, this hurts the fans who may not support the use of CGI on these actors.
Are these impacts intended or unintended? How do you know? How do you know?
The positive ones are intended as not having to hire a new actor may be the key to keeping a show from changing. The negative ones are unintended, however inevitable. These impacts are unintended. I know this because there is no reason for the directors to want a negative impact on their own work, as it was bring down the value of the film.
